Wildfires in Colorado

According to the National Interagency Fire Center, in 2021 there were a total of 337 recorded wildfires in colorado. A total of 42,202 acres of land faced damages, and 1,102 buildings were destroyed.

Fort Lyon

The Fort Lyon fire took place in Bent County. The fire started on January 15th and was contained on January 16th, it burnt a total of 1,433 acres of land. The fire ignited as a result of dry land and was spread by the wind.

Oil Springs

The Oil Springs fire took place in Rio Blanco County. The fire started on June 18th and was contained on July 10th, it burnt a total of 12,613 acres of land. The fire was believed to have started due to lightning.

West

The West fire took place in Moffat County. The fire started on June 20th and was contained on July 1st, it burnt a total of 3,429 acres of land. The fire was believed to have started due to lightning.

Muddy Slide

The Muddy Slide fire took place in Routt County. The fire started on June 20th and was contained on September 14th, it burnt a total of 4,093 acres of land. The fire was believed to have started due to lightning. 18 structures were destroyed.

Sylvan

The Sylvan fire took place in Eagle County. The fire started on June 20th and was contained on October 14th, it burnt a total of 3,792 acres of land. The fire was believed to have started due to lightning.

Morgan Creek

The Morgan Creek fire took place in Routt County. The fire started on July 10th and was contained on October 14th, it burnt a total of 7,586 acres of land. The fire was believed to have started due to lightning.

Bijou

The Bijou fire took place in Morgan County. The fire started on Jaugust 27th and was contained on August 28th, it burnt a total of 3,083 acres of land. The fire was believed to have started due to lightning.

Kruger Rock

The Kruger Rock fire took place in Larimer County. The fire started on November 16th and was contained on November 20th, it burnt a total of 147 acres of land. The fire was started by winds blowing a tre onto a powerline. There was one fatality.

Marshall Fire

The Marshal Fire took place in Boulder County. The fire started on December 30th and was contained on January 1st, it burnt a total of 6,026 acres of land. The cause is still unknown. 1,084 structures were destroyed, 149 structures were damaged, there was one fatality, and six injuries.
